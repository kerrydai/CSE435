Lane Management System

Project Background

More and more modern cars are adding safety features. With the usage of cameras detecting the lane lines, the system can now assist drivers in driving.

Project Description

The Lane Management System is an automated driver assistance system designed to detect lanes and assist or warn drivers to stay within the lane.

Meeting Summary

Meeting date and time: 11/24/2025 10:00PM

Meeting Length: 1.5 hours

Personnel: Entire team

Platform: Discord

Summary:

1. Review + visualize each prototype scenarios for the SRS
a. Scenario 2:
i. Group confirmed that Scenario 2 should visually demonstrate how LMS handles roads with multiple curvature segments.
ii. LMS must predict curvature using available lane information and maintain lane centering throughout varying curve intensities.
iii. Team agreed the prototype should include both mild and strong curvature to stress the system and highlight LMS steering adjustments.
b. Scenario 3:
i. Prototype will demonstrate a situation where lane boundaries disappear, triggering LMS shutdown.
ii. On-screen text must clearly communicate:
1. LMS transitioning ON → OFF, and
2. LDWS issuing a system disabled warning.
iii. This scenario will emphasize LMS's safety behavior when critical lane information becomes unreliable.
c. Scenario 4:
i. Prototype will use three on-screen indicators: LDWS, Turn Signal, and LMS.
ii. Vehicle approaches lane boundary LDWS shows Lane Approach Warning.
iii. After 2 seconds driver activates turn signal LMS enters Standby, LDWS Disabled, Turn Signal Active.
iv. Driver crosses lane, centers vehicle, turns off signal → indicators revert to initial states.
2. Discuss proposed updates to the Domain Model/Sequence diagram changes
a. Team reviewed the suggestion to introduce a User Interface class through which turn signal, torque sensor, and other subsystem messages would route before reaching LMS.
b. We acknowledged that this would improve clarity and simplify the sequence diagrams conceptually.
c. However, the team ultimately decided not to update the domain model this late in the assignment cycle because:
i. Changing the domain model would require reworking the entire data dictionary,
ii. All sequence diagrams would need to be updated to reflect UI-mediated interactions, and
iii. These changes would introduce significant risk and workload given the submission timeline.
iv. Graded based off the feedback from TA + customer + other group
3. Go over customer feedback
a. The team went through all client clarifications received earlier in the day, including:
i. Lane curvature may come from GPS/map data and cross-checks from the vehicle state estimation system.
ii. LCS will also apply steering control at a minimal, safe threshold.
iii. LCS/LKS may signal the Warning System to issue relevant alerts.
iv. User Interface (UI) should support driver inputs (e.g., enabling LMS) and display warnings back to the driver.
b. Additional customer feedback on the SRS and prototype included:
i. Prototype link was not runnable; screenshots lacked clarity regarding scenario behavior.
ii. Clarification needed on whether the driver retains control over LKS independently of LMS.
iii. Alerts and warning conditions need to be more explicitly linked to system states in the use case diagram.
iv. Fig. 8 turn-signal scenario raised confusion—does enabling the turn signal immediately stop correction? If so, is that intended?
v. Requested clarification on the distinction between centering vs. correcting states in the Electronic Steering state diagram, including how driver torque affects transitions.
vi. General request to improve diagram clarity, font size, and resolution.
